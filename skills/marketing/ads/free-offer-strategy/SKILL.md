# Free Offer Strategy

## Overview

Design free offers -- quizzes, guides, mini-courses, replays, audio series -- that solve a real problem, qualify interest, and create a natural bridge into webinars and paid programs. Use this skill when planning any top-of-funnel free content for the organization.

## Core Principles

1. **Solve a real problem.** The free offer must provide genuine value on its own. Someone who never buys should still benefit.
2. **Adjacent to the paid experience.** The offer should naturally lead toward what the organization sells. If someone completes it, the next logical step is a webinar or program.
3. **Simple and focused.** One clear outcome per offer. No feature creep. Immediate value on first interaction.
4. **Voice integrity.** Free offers carry the same `voice-calibration` standards as paid content. No "lead magnet" energy, no hype, no manufactured urgency. The free offer IS the first taste of the transformation container.

## Free Offer Archetypes

### 1. Readiness Assessment / Quiz
- Interactive, branching questions (5-12 questions max)
- Personalized result page with specific recommendations
- Works because: people want to know where they stand; personalized output feels high-value
- Example angle: "How ready are you for a psychedelic healing experience?"

### 2. Downloadable Guide
- PDF or web-based structured framework
- Preparation checklists, decision frameworks, educational primers
- Works because: tangible takeaway, reference material people return to
- Example angle: "The Preparation Guide: What to Know Before Your First Ceremony"

### 3. Mini-Course (Video or Email Drip)
- 3-7 days, one lesson per day
- Each lesson builds on the previous; final lesson bridges to webinar/program
- Works because: daily touchpoints build trust and habit; completion signals high intent
- Example angle: "5 Days of Nervous System Regulation" (daily practice + teaching)

### 4. Recorded Workshop Replay
- Repurpose an existing webinar or live session
- Add context (intro/outro) explaining what comes next
- Works because: demonstrates the quality of live experiences; low creation cost
- Example angle: Replay of a past Have a Good Trip session with post-watch CTA

### 5. Audio Series
- Guided meditations, breathwork sessions, regulation practices
- 3-5 episodes, delivered via email or private podcast feed
- Works because: experiential (not just informational); builds somatic trust
- Example angle: "3 Guided Practices for Pre-Ceremony Preparation"

## Ideation Framework

Follow these steps to generate and validate offer ideas:

1. **Identify audience pain points**
   - What questions do people ask before committing to a retreat or program?
   - What fears or uncertainties prevent them from taking the next step?
   - What do past participants wish they had known beforehand?

2. **Validate demand**
   - Search social media and community forums for recurring questions
   - Review intake form responses and sales call notes for patterns
   - Check if competitors or adjacent practitioners offer something similar
   - Ask: "Would someone share this with a friend who is curious but not ready?"

3. **Select archetype**
   - Match the pain point to the archetype that best delivers the answer
   - Prefer interactive (quiz, mini-course) over static (PDF) when possible
   - Consider creation effort vs. expected engagement

## Lead Capture Strategy

### Gating by Archetype

| Archetype | Recommended Gating | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Quiz | Email required for personalized result | Result creates urgency to check email; high capture rate |
| Guide | Email required for delivery | Deliver via email sequence over 2-3 days, not instant download -- builds relationship |
| Mini-course | Email required for enrollment | Daily drip emails build habit and trust |
| Workshop replay | Email required for access link | Time-limited access creates urgency |
| Audio series | Email required for episode delivery | Drip delivery mirrors mini-course pattern |

### Gating Options (choose per offer)

- **Fully gated**: Email required before any access. Use for high-value, personalized content (quizzes, mini-courses).
- **Partial preview**: Show first lesson/question/chapter free, gate the rest. Good for guides and audio series.
- **Optional gate**: Content accessible without email; email unlocks bonus (saved results, extra content). Maximizes reach.
- **Ungated with CTA**: No gate at all. Pure brand play. Include clear next-step CTA throughout.

### Post-Capture Sequence

1. Immediate: deliver the promised content (result, first lesson, download link)
2. Days 1-3: deliver remaining content if dripped
3. Day 3-5: bridge email -- connect what they learned to the live experience (webinar or info session)
4. Day 5-7: direct invitation to upcoming webinar or program with specific date/link
5. All emails follow `voice-calibration` standards. No hard sell. The bridge should feel like a natural next step.

## Offer-to-Webinar Bridge

Design the bridge at offer creation time, not after. Every free offer must answer: "What is the single next step this person should take?"

- **Quiz**: Result page includes "Join our next live session to explore your result" + webinar link
- **Guide**: Final section titled "What Comes Next" with webinar invitation
- **Mini-course**: Final lesson is the bridge lesson -- summarize what they learned, introduce what the live experience offers that self-study cannot
- **Replay**: Post-watch page with "Attend the next live session" CTA
- **Audio series**: Final episode includes spoken invitation to go deeper in a live container

The bridge should never feel like a bait-and-switch. The free offer must stand on its own. The bridge is an invitation, not a pitch.

## Evaluation Scorecard

Rate each factor 1-5 before committing to build:

| Factor | Score (1-5) |
|---|---|
| **Audience resonance** -- does this match a felt need? | ___ |
| **Transformation adjacency** -- how close to the paid experience? | ___ |
| **Ease of creation** -- can this be built in days, not months? | ___ |
| **Shareability** -- would someone send this to a curious friend? | ___ |
| **Lead quality signal** -- does completing this indicate readiness? | ___ |

- **20-25**: Strong candidate. Proceed to MVP.
- **13-19**: Promising. Refine the concept or narrow scope.
- **Below 13**: Reconsider. The offer may not pull its weight.

## MVP Scope

Ship the smallest version that delivers real value:

1. **Core content only** -- the one thing the offer promises, delivered well
2. **Essential UX** -- clear input, obvious output, works on mobile
3. **Basic lead capture** -- email collection works, leads flow to Attio, follow-up sequence exists
4. **Skip initially**: account creation, saved results, advanced personalization, perfect design

Iterate based on data after launch, not before.

## Promotion Strategy

1. **Owned channels first**
   - Announce to existing email list
   - Share on social media (Instagram, community groups)
   - Feature in existing community spaces and alumni networks
   - Add to website navigation or landing page

2. **Paid amplification** (cross-reference `paid-ads` skill if available)
   - Run targeted ads to the offer landing page
   - Retarget website visitors who did not convert

3. **Partner and community**
   - Share with aligned practitioners, facilitators, or organizations
   - Guest features in relevant newsletters or podcasts

## Measurement

Track three layers:

1. **Acquisition**: signups / downloads / quiz starts
2. **Engagement**: completion rate (quiz finished, guide read, course completed, all episodes listened)
3. **Conversion to next step**: webinar registrations, program inquiries, or paid enrollments attributed to this offer

Review metrics 2 weeks after launch. If completion rate is below 40%, simplify the offer. If conversion to next step is below 5%, strengthen the bridge.

## Steps

1. Clarify the goal: what paid program or webinar does this offer feed into?
2. Identify the audience pain point the offer will address (use ideation framework above)
3. Select the archetype that best fits the pain point and available content
4. Score the idea using the evaluation scorecard -- proceed only if 13+
5. Define MVP scope: core content, lead capture mechanism, follow-up sequence
6. Design the offer-to-webinar bridge before building the offer itself
7. Write or produce the content following `voice-calibration` standards
8. Set up lead capture (email gate) and connect to Attio via `attio` skill
9. Build the follow-up email sequence via `resend` skill
10. Launch to owned channels first; add paid promotion after initial data

## Error Handling

- If the user has not identified a paid program or webinar to bridge to: ask them to define the next step first. A free offer without a bridge is just content, not a funnel element.
- If the scorecard total is below 13: flag it and recommend refining before building.
- If no audience pain point is clear: suggest reviewing intake forms, sales call transcripts, or community questions before proceeding.
