# Copy Editing

## Overview

Use this skill after drafting to systematically polish any the organization marketing copy before review. The Eight Sweeps framework runs eight sequential passes, each focused on one dimension, catching issues that get missed in a single unfocused review.

## The Eight Sweeps Framework

Run all eight sweeps in order. After each sweep, verify that edits did not compromise earlier sweeps.

---

### Sweep 1: Clarity

**Focus:** Can the reader understand what you are saying on first read?

**Check for:**
- Sentences trying to hold more than one idea
- Unclear pronoun references
- Insider language or jargon without context
- Ambiguous statements that could be read two ways
- Missing context the reader needs

**Process:**
1. Read through once, flagging unclear passages without correcting
2. For each flag, rewrite to express one idea per sentence
3. Front-load the important information in each sentence
4. Verify rewrites preserve the original intent

**After this sweep:** Confirm the Rule of One (one main idea per section) and that every sentence speaks directly to the reader.

---

### Sweep 2: Voice and Tone

**Focus:** Does the copy sound like the organization, and does the register match the piece type?

**Check for:**
- Shifts between formal and intimate register within a section
- Words from the Brand Language Kill List still present
- Corporate or clinical language where warmth belongs
- Inconsistent use of we/you/I (see voice-calibration E5)

**Brand Kill List check (mandatory):**

| If you find | Replace with |
|---|---|
| Sacred | Meaningful / Intentional / Ritual |
| Miracle | Lasting Change / Real Shift / Genuine Transformation |
| Therapy / Therapeutic | Healing Experience / Inner Work / Facilitated Journey |
| Spirit Guide | Facilitator / Guide / Practitioner |
| Breakthrough | Realization / Opening / Integration / Recognition |

**Process:**
1. Read aloud (or mentally at speaking pace) to hear inconsistencies
2. Flag every kill-list word and replace
3. Mark where tone shifts unexpectedly and smooth transitions
4. Verify brand personality holds throughout

**After this sweep:** Return to Clarity to ensure voice edits did not introduce confusion.

---

### Sweep 3: So What

**Focus:** Every paragraph must earn its place. The reader should feel "this matters to me."

**The test:** For every statement, ask "Okay, so what?" If the copy does not answer with a deeper benefit or meaning, it needs work.

**Common failures:**
- Features listed without connecting to what they mean for the reader
- Impressive-sounding claims that do not land in the reader's life
- Program details without answering why the reader should care

**Example:**
- Weak: "Our retreats include integration sessions."
- Strong: "After the ceremony, you will not be left alone with what surfaced. Integration sessions give you space to make sense of it, with people who understand."

**Process:**
1. Read each claim and literally ask "so what?"
2. Flag claims missing the answer
3. Add the benefit bridge or deeper meaning
4. Ensure benefits connect to felt desires, not abstract outcomes

**After this sweep:** Return to Voice/Tone, then Clarity.

---

### Sweep 4: Prove It

**Focus:** Claims need evidence. Flag anything unsubstantiated.

**Check for:**
- Assertions without backup ("transformative experience" with no proof)
- Superlatives without evidence ("the most," "the best")
- Results claims without specifics
- Testimonial references that lack names, details, or attribution

**Types of proof available to the organization:**
- Participant testimonials (use Senja skill to fetch real ones)
- Alumni count and retreat count (400+ alumni, 60+ retreats)
- Research citations (Johns Hopkins, NYU — never MAPS)
- Facilitator credentials and experience
- Specific participant outcomes (with permission)

**Process:**
1. Identify every claim that needs proof
2. Check if supporting evidence exists nearby in the copy
3. Flag unsupported assertions
4. Recommend adding proof, or softening the claim if proof is unavailable

**After this sweep:** Return to So What, Voice/Tone, then Clarity.

---

### Sweep 5: Specificity

**Focus:** Replace generalities with concrete details.

**Check for:**
- Vague language: "improve," "enhance," "optimize," "many people"
- Generic statements that could describe any retreat company
- Missing numbers, timeframes, or concrete examples
- Adjectives doing the work that nouns or details should do

**the organization specificity upgrades:**

| Vague | Specific |
|---|---|
| Many participants | 400+ alumni across 60+ retreats |
| Experienced facilitators | Facilitators with 10+ years in expanded-state work |
| A supportive community | A lifelong community — alumni who text each other at 2am |
| Stress reduction | The exhale you have been holding for years finally arrives |

**Apply voice-calibration E2:** Replace category words (stress, anxiety, burnout) with the specific felt experience. "Stress" becomes "the weight that has been sitting behind your sternum." "Burnout" becomes "the exhaustion that does not come from doing too much — it comes from doing too much of the wrong thing."

**Process:**
1. Flag every vague word or phrase
2. Replace with a number, a timeframe, a sensory detail, or a concrete example
3. Remove content that cannot be made specific — it is likely filler
4. Verify replacements still read naturally at speaking pace

**After this sweep:** Return to Prove It, So What, Voice/Tone, then Clarity.

---

### Sweep 6: Heightened Emotion

**Focus:** Verify emotional intensity matches the piece type.

**Use the voice-calibration V5 register table:**

| Piece Type | Register |
|---|---|
| Integration email (post-retreat) | Quiet, grounded, tender — they are in the afterglow |
| Launch close | Present, urgent, direct — the moment is now |
| Nurture email | Warm, exploratory, curious — no pressure, just proximity |
| Retreat description | Expansive, contemplative, inviting — possibility without pressure |
| Newsletter intro | Intimate, conversational, personal — like a letter |
| Blog / thought leadership | Measured, bridging, authoritative but not above |
| Social post | Distilled, poetic, single-idea — one breath |

**Check for:**
- Flat, informational language where emotion should live
- Emotional intensity that overshoots the register (e.g., urgent language in a nurture email)
- Pain points mentioned but not felt — named as categories instead of experiences
- Aspirations stated abstractly instead of evoked sensorily

**Process:**
1. Identify the piece type and its correct register
2. Read for emotional impact — does it move you appropriately for this register?
3. Flag sections where intensity is too high or too low
4. Adjust emotional texture while staying authentic to the register

**After this sweep:** Return to Specificity, Prove It, So What, Voice/Tone, then Clarity.

---

### Sweep 7: Zero Risk

**Focus:** Remove anything that could create legal, brand, or trust risk.

**Check for:**
- Health outcome claims ("cures," "treats," "heals depression")
- Specific substance names in general audience content (use "plant medicine" or "expanded states")
- Clinical claims that position the organization as a medical provider
- Promises of specific results that cannot be guaranteed
- Language that contradicts the organization's 508(c)(1)(a) church entity status
- "Licensed Healing Center" (prohibited — use "licensed facilitators" or "licensed natural medicine facilitators")
- Any reference to MAPS or Rick Doblin (prohibited — use "FDA Breakthrough Therapy designation" or "Johns Hopkins / NYU research")
- Fine print or qualifiers that create doubt near CTAs
- Missing trust signals near calls to action

**Process:**
1. Flag every health, outcome, or clinical claim
2. Soften or remove claims that cannot be substantiated
3. Check all substance references against audience type
4. Verify trust signals (proof, safety language, clear next steps) appear near every CTA
5. Confirm no prohibited entity or partnership references remain

**After this sweep:** Return through all previous sweeps one final time.

---

### Sweep 8: the organization Voice

**Focus:** Final pass through voice-calibration E1-E5 to ensure the copy carries the active brand signature.

**E1 — Reader on the journey, not broken:**
Does any sentence imply the reader is broken, lost, damaged, or in need of saving? Rewrite to meet them mid-stride. "You've been sensing that something deeper is possible" — not "If you're struggling, we can help."

**E2 — Interior precision, not categories:**
Are interior experiences described with precision or with category labels? "The quiet suspicion that you have been optimizing your life for someone else's definition of it" — not "stress and anxiety."

**E3 — Ceremony container in language:**
Does the writing carry reverence without solemnity? Does it leave space around the ineffable? Gesture, do not narrate what happens in ceremony.

**E4 — Bridge science and spirit:**
Where research or data appears, does it land in human experience? "What those studies measure is the threshold of something that cannot be measured."

**E5 — Communal first-person plural:**
Is "we" used as the community of pilgrims (correct) or as the corporate brand voice (incorrect)? Is "you" intimate and direct? Is "I" reserved for the operator's personal story only?

**Process:**
1. Read the full draft once for each E-rule (five mini-passes)
2. Flag every violation with the rule number
3. Provide a rewrite for each flag
4. After all five, read once more to confirm the piece could not have been written by any other wellness brand — if it could, deepen the voice

## Quick-Pass Checks

Use these for fast reviews when a full eight-sweep process is not needed.

### Word Level
- Cut weak intensifiers: very, really, extremely, incredibly
- Cut filler: just, actually, basically, in order to
- Replace corporate words: utilize -> use, leverage -> use, facilitate -> help, robust -> strong, seamless -> smooth, innovative -> new
- Watch for: unnecessary adverbs, passive voice, nominalizations (verb turned into noun: "make a decision" -> "decide")

### Sentence Level
- One idea per sentence
- Vary sentence length (mix short and long — poet's rhythm per V4)
- Front-load important information
- Max 25 words per sentence as a guideline
- No more than 3 conjunctions per sentence

### Paragraph Level
- One topic per paragraph
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences for web copy)
- Strong opening sentences
- Logical flow between paragraphs
- White space for scannability

## Common Problems and Fixes

**Wall of Program Details:**
Symptom: listing what the retreat includes without connecting to why it matters.
Fix: after each detail, add "which means..." to bridge to the felt benefit.

**Corporate Wellness Speak:**
Symptom: "Leverage our transformative modalities to optimize your wellbeing."
Fix: ask "How would the operator say this to someone sitting across from him?" Use those words.

**Weak Opening:**
Symptom: starting with the organization, the retreat, or what is being sold.
Fix: open in the reader's interior world (V1). Their felt experience, their unnamed longing.

**No Proof:**
Symptom: "Participants love our retreats" with no evidence.
Fix: add a specific testimonial, a number, or a concrete detail. "400+ alumni. Many return."

**Generic Claims:**
Symptom: "We help people heal and grow."
Fix: specify who, how, and what changes. "High-performing founders who arrived carrying the weight of everyone else's expectations — and left remembering what their own felt like."

**Mixed Register:**
Symptom: copy starts intimate and contemplative, then shifts to urgent sales language.
Fix: identify the correct register from V5 and hold it throughout.

## Steps

1. Receive the draft and identify the piece type (email, landing page, social post, etc.) and target reader
2. If piece type or audience is unclear, ask before proceeding
3. Run all eight sweeps in order: Clarity, Voice/Tone, So What, Prove It, Specificity, Heightened Emotion, Zero Risk, the organization Voice
4. For each issue found, quote the original sentence, name the sweep and rule violated, and provide the rewrite
5. After completing all sweeps, do one final read to confirm no sweep introduced new problems
6. Return the annotated draft

## Output Format

For each flag:
```
> [quoted original sentence]
[Sweep N: Rule name] — [brief explanation of the issue]
Rewrite: [improved sentence]
```

After all flags, provide a summary:
- Total flags by sweep
- Overall voice assessment: does this piece carry the active brand signature?
- One-line note for the operator's review: what was prioritized, what to watch for

## Error Handling

- If the piece type is not specified: ask the user before running sweeps (register selection in Sweep 6 depends on piece type)
- If the draft contains no clear target audience: ask, or default to avatar C1 (Quiet Crossroads Professional) as the most common the organization reader
- If the draft is under 50 words: run Quick-Pass Checks only, skip the full eight-sweep process
